Some Facts about Iconic Things in Egypt

Luxor

  • Location: Luxor, on the east bank of the Nile, near the Luxor Temple. The museum sits on a hilltop offering views of the city and river.
  • Opening: The museum opened to the public in 1975. It was built to house and display artifacts from the Theban temples and tombs.
  • Focus: Specializes in the Theban collection, particularly items from the Valley of the Kings, the temples at Karnak, and other New Kingdom sites. It also includes artifacts from earlier periods.

Aswan

  • Location and geography:Located on the eastern bank of the Nile near where the river cuts through the granite outcrops of the Aswan Nubian region. It sits at the first cataract of the Nile and serves as a gateway to Upper Egypt.
  • Historical significance:A key ancient trade and riverine crossing point between Africa and Egypt; the name "Aswan" comes from the Arabic "Aswan," derived from the ancient Egyptian site name "Senen." Nearby sites include the Philae Temple complex (island temple dedicated to Isis) and the Aswan Dam.
  • Modern landmarks:Aswan High Dam: Completed in the 1960s, it controls Nile flooding, provides hydroelectric power, and created a large reservoir (Lake Nasser) to the south. Elephantine Island: A historic Nile island with ancient archaeological sites and traditional Nubian communities. Philae Temple: A renowned temple complex relocated to Agilkia Island after the original site flooded by the Aswan High Dam.
